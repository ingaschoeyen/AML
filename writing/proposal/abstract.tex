\section{Abstract}
Only during the last fraction of the existence of civil engineering have digital archives been a thing. 
When the digitalisation started around 60 years ago, older documents were scanned and described in local archives.
Now, when the provinces' civil engineers need to find relevant documents they must schour these archives by hand in an intensive process.
We set out to develop a system where these documents are processed, described, and made ready to be searched on trough a natural language interface.
Leveraging state-of-the-art machine learning techniques, we aim to develop a system that consists mostly of local, open-source solutions to give the province, and anyone else with great old archives, a maintainable and secure architecture for archival search.
